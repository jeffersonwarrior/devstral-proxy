# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful**: Treat others with dignity and respect
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome and encourage all participants
- **Be constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and focus on improving the project
- **Be collaborative**: Work together as a community
- **Be professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Offensive comments, sexual language, unwanted attention
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or actions
- **Personal attacks**: Insults, demeaning comments, or personal criticism
- **Spam**: Posting irrelevant content or repeatedly posting the same content
- **Trolling**: Deliberately provoking or upsetting others

## Responsibilities

### Contributors

- **Be welcoming**: Help new contributors feel included
- **Be helpful**: Provide constructive feedback and guidance
- **Be patient**: Understand that everyone has different levels of experience
- **Be respectful**: Treat all community members with respect

### Maintainers

- **Be fair**: Apply standards consistently across the community
- **Be responsive**: Address issues and concerns promptly
- **Be transparent**: Communicate openly about decisions and policies
- **Be supportive**: Help contributors grow and improve

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub repositories**: Issues, pull requests, discussions
- **Documentation**: README files, wiki pages, code comments
- **Communication**: Emails, chat, forums, social media
- **Events**: Online and offline meetings, conferences

### Private Interactions

While private interactions are not covered by this policy, we encourage respectful behavior in all contexts.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

To report violations:
1. **Contact maintainers**: Email the project maintainers
2. **Use GitHub reporting**: Use GitHub's reporting tools
3. **Private message**: Send a direct message to a maintainer

### Process

1. **Investigation**: Maintainers will review the report
2. **Action**: Appropriate action will be taken
3. **Follow-up**: The reporter will be informed of actions taken

### Consequences

Violations may result in:
- **Warning**: Private warning and education
- **Temporary suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent ban**: Permanent removal from community spaces
